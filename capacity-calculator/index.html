What it is

Built for the headcount conversation

Most awareness practitioners can feel that they are stretched. Fewer can show it clearly. This tool helps you map your current hours across the activities that actually make up your week, then model what changes when a new hire joins — or when your role shifts.

Set your weekly target hours, your new hire's target hours, and your own target after the hire — three independent columns, no auto-linking between them. Allocate each activity across all three and watch the donut charts, the team capacity total, and the plain-English narrative update in real time. A guided setup wizard walks you through the whole thing in about five minutes, including a category picker so you only see the activities that apply to your role. A business case section connects the numbers to outcomes your organisation actually cares about.

No installation. No login. Nothing leaves your browser.

How it works

Use the wizard or dive straight in

1

Pick your activities

The wizard opens on first visit and starts by asking what your week actually involves. Select from 12 practitioner-specific categories, deselect anything that does not apply, add your own if something is missing, and flag any capabilities that don't exist yet but would become possible with more capacity.

2

Name your pressure points

Select the statements that ring true — content creation is a bottleneck, reactive work crowds out planning, reporting eats hours you'd rather spend elsewhere. These feed straight into the risk section of your business case later.

3

Set your weekly hours

Enter how many hours you work per week, not including lunch. This becomes your baseline. Quick-select options cover the most common contracts.

4

Describe the new hire's role

A sentence or two on what they would take off your plate. This becomes the "why this role matters" text on the new hire slide.

5

Allocate your current time

Drag the sliders, or type the hours directly, to show how your week is split across your chosen activities. A live total in the header tracks progress against your target.

6

Build the business case

Answer four questions — the initiative this investment unlocks, the risk it addresses, what success looks like in 12 months, and how you will measure it. Your pain points pre-fill the risk answer if you leave it blank.

Once you finish, the calculator opens with everything in place. From there you can independently fine-tune three columns — your time now, your time after the hire, and the new hire's time — and export a four-slide presentation, individual donut charts, a full Excel workbook, a PNG image, or a printed PDF.
